Key ideas

Product rule

\[\begin{align} \frac{d}{dx}[u(x)\times v(x)]&=u\times Dv+v\times Du \end{align}\]

Critical values rules

  1. \(\frac{dy}{dx}=0\)
  2. \(f'(x)=\text{does not exist}\)
  3. Endpoints of a given domain
  • If there is a product of two different functions, use the product rule.
    • Example: an \(x\) in two places separated by \(\times\) (multiplication) or \(\div\) (division).

Steps for using the product rule:

  1. Identify if there is a product between two function,s and if there is, label \(U\) and \(V\).
  2. Find and simplify the derivatives of \(U\) and \(V\).
  3. Substitute in all values and terms.
  4. Simplify the derivative using various techniques like common factors, common denominators, reduing, etc.

Sample problems

  1. If \(f(x)=x^2e^{-\frac{x}{2}}\), find \(f'(x)\).

    2. Finding individual derivatives

    \[\begin{align} u&=x^2\to 2x \\ v&=e^{-\frac{x}{2}}\to-\frac{1}{2}e^{-\frac{x}{2}} \end{align}\]

    Placing parts together

    \[\begin{align} f'(x)&=x^2(-\frac{1}{2}e^{-\frac{x}{2}})+e^{-\frac{x}{2}}(2x) \\ &=e^{-\frac{x}{2}}x(-\frac{1}{2}x+2) \end{align}\]
  2. \(\frac{d}{dx}[x^2\sin x]\)

    3. Finding individual derivatives

    \[\begin{align} u&=x^2 \to 2x \\ v&=\sin x \to -\cos x \end{align}\]

    Placing parts together

    \[\begin{align} \frac{dy}{dx}&=x^2(-\cos x)+\sin x(2x) \\ &=x(-x\cos x + 2\sin x) \\ \end{align}\]
  3. \(\frac{d}{dx}[(2x-3)^8(3x^2-1)^7]\)

    4. Finding individual derivatives

    \[\begin{align} u&=(2x-3)^8 \to 8(2x-3)^7\times 2 \\ v&=(3x^2-1)^7 \to 7(3x^2-1)^6\times 6x \end{align}\]

    Placing parts together

    \[\begin{align} \frac{du}{dx}&=(2x-3)^8\times 42x(3x^2-1)^6+(3x^2-1)^7\times 16(2x-3)^7 \\ &=2(2x-3)^7(3x^2-1)^6(66^2-63x-8) \end{align}\]
  4. Find the critical values of \(y=x\sqrt{1-x^2}\).

    5. First test

    \[\begin{align} 1-2x^2&=0 \\ -2x^2&=-1 \\ x^2&=\frac{1}{2} \\ x&=\pm\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \end{align}\]

    Second test

    \[\begin{align} (1-x^2)^{\frac{1}{2}}&=0 \\ x&=\pm1 \end{align}\]

    Third test

    No endpoints

    Critical values

    \[\begin{align} x&=\pm\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}, \pm1 \end{align}\]
