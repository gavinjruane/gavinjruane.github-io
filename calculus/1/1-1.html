Transcendentals and derivatives 18 May 2022

Key ideas

Trigonometric rules

\[\begin{align} \frac{d}{dx}[\sin u]&=(\cos u)\frac{du}{dx} \\ \frac{d}{dx}[\cos u]&=(-\sin u)\frac{du}{dx} \\ \frac{d}{dx}[\tan u]&=(\sec ^2 u)\frac{du}{dx} \\ \frac{d}{dx}[\csc u]&=(-\csc u \cot u)\frac{du}{dx} \\ \frac{d}{dx}[\sec u]&=(\sec u \tan u)\frac{du}{dx} \\ \frac{d}{dx}[\cot u]&=(-\csc ^2 u)\frac{du}{dx} \end{align}\]

Logarithmic rules

\[\begin{align} \frac{d}{dx}[\ln u]&=(\frac{1}{u})\frac{du}{dx} \\ \frac{d}{dx}[\log _a u]&=(\frac{1}{u\times \ln a})\frac{du}{dx} \\ \end{align}\]
  • The derivative is the slope.
  • The rules are essential to understanding different types of derivatives.

Sample problems

  1. \(\displaystyle{\frac{d}{dx}(\sin^3 x)}\)
    2. \[\begin{align} &=\frac{d}{dx}(\sin x)^3 \\ \frac{dy}{dx}&=3(\sin x)^2 \times (\cos x) \end{align}\]
  2. \(\displaystyle{\frac{d}{dx}(\sin x^3)}\)
    3. \[\begin{align} \frac{dy}{dx}&=-\cos (x^3)\times 3x^2 \end{align}\]
  3. \(\displaystyle{\frac{d}{dx}(\ln 4x^2)}\)
    4. \[\begin{align} \frac{dy}{dx}&=\frac{1}{4x^2}(8x) \\ &=\frac{2}{x} \end{align}\]
  4. \(\displaystyle{f(x)=\sqrt[5]{\ln x}}\)
    5. \[\begin{align} &=(\ln x)^{\frac{1}{5}} \\ f'(x)&=\frac{1}{5}(\ln x)^{-\frac{4}{5}}\times\frac{1}{x}\times 1 \\ &=\frac{1}{5x(\ln x)^{\frac{4}{5}}} \end{align}\]
