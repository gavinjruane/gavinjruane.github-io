Calculus Reference

Algebraic Expressions

Algebraic expression: A polynomial of the variable \(x\) is an expression of \[a_nx^n+a_(n-1)x^(n-1)+\dots+a_1x+a_0\] where \(a_0\), \(a_1\), etc. are real numbers and \(n\) is non-negative. If \(a_n\neq0\) the degree is \(n\).

FOIL: First term → Outer → Inner → Last

  1. Add and subtract polynomials term-by-term.
    2. Addition
    \[\begin{align}&(x^3-6x^2+2x+4)+(x^3+5x^2-7x) \\ &(x^3+x^3)+(-6x^2+5x^2)+(2x-7x)+4 \\ &2x^3-x^2-5x+4 &\end{align}\]
    Subtraction

    Notice that you have to distribute the negative sign.

    \[\begin{align}&(x^3-6x^2+2x+4)-(x^3+5x^2-7x) \\ &(x^3-6x^2+2x+4)+(-x^3-5x^2+7x) \\ &-11x^2+9x+4 &\end{align}\]
  2. Multiply using two methods—either the FOIL or Box method.
