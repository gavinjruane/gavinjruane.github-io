Continuity 17 May 2022

Key ideas

Principles of continuity

A function, \(f(x)\), is continuous at \(x=a\) if and only if

  1. \(f(x)\) exists when \(f(a)=a\) is a real value
  2. \(\lim\limits_{x \to a}f(x)\) exists (\((x \to a^+)=(x \to a^-)\))
  3. \(f(a)=\lim\limits_{x \to a}f(x)\)

Sample problems

  1. Prove \(f(x)\) is continuous at \(x=1\).

    2. \(f(x)=\left\{ \begin{array}{ll} x^2+6x-5 & x<1 \\ x+1 & x\geq 1 \end{array} \right.\)

    1. \[\begin{align} f(1)&=(1)+1 \\ &=2 \end{align}\]

      2. Great! \(f(1)\) is a real number. We can move on.

    2. \[\begin{align} \lim\limits_{x \to 1^+}x+1&=\lim\limits_{x \to 1^-}x^2+6x-5 \\ (1)+1&=(1)^2+6(1)-5 \\ 2&=2 \end{align}\]

      3. The limits from the left and the right are equal. That's perfect! We can move on.

    3. \[\begin{align} f(1)&=\lim\limits_{x \to 1}f(x) \\ 2&=2 \end{align}\]

      4. \(f(1)\) is equal to the value of the limits from the left and the right! That's great! This problem is continuous!

    \(f(x)\) is continuous at \(x=1\).

  2. Is \(g(x)\) continuous at \(x=0\)?

    3. \(g(x)=\left\{ \begin{array}{ll} x^2-5 & x\leq 0 \\ x+2 & x>0 \end{array} \right.\)

    1. \[\begin{align} g(0)&=(0)^2-5\\ &=-5 \end{align}\]
    2. \[\begin{align} \lim\limits_{x \to 0^-}x^2-5&=\lim\limits_{x \to 0^+}x+2 \\ -5&\neq2 \end{align}\]

    3. Not necessary to complete.

    \(g(x)\) is not continuous at \(x=0\).
