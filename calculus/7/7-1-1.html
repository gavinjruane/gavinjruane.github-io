Key ideas

The limit definition

Given \(f(x)\), then \(f'(x)=\lim\limits_{h \to 0}\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}\).

  • \(H\) represents the difference between two ppints
  • If you see an \(h\), find the derivative of the limit's function.

Steps for evaluating a limit with \(h\):

  1. Change any instance of \((x+h)\) or \((a+h)\) to \(x\).
  2. Find the derivative of these changed terms in the function. Don't include the rest of the function in your derivative work.
  3. If the original term was not \((x+h)\) and had a constant \((a+h)\): substitute that constant in for \(x\) and solve.

Sample problems

  1. \(\displaystyle{\lim\limits_{h \to 0}\frac{3(x+h)^2-5(x+h)-3x^2+5x}{h}}\)
    2. \[\begin{align} \frac{dy}{dx}&=3(x)^2-5(x) \\ &=6x=5 \end{align}\]

    See how we changed the terms \(3(x+h)^2\) and \(-5(x+h)\) and then took the derivative of those changed forms? That's what you'll need to do for these problems.

  2. \(\displaystyle{\lim\limits_{h \to 0}\frac{4(2+h)^2-3(2+h)+18}{h}}\)
    3. \[\begin{align} \frac{dy}{dx}&=4(x)^2-3(x) \\ &=8x-3 \end{align}\]

    That's great, but we need to substitute in the constant now.

    \[\begin{align} &=8(2)-3 \\ &=13 \end{align}\]
  3. \(\displaystyle{\lim\limits_{h \to 0}\frac{4(x+h)^{-1}+\sin (x+h)-4x^{-1}-\sin x}{h}}\)
    4. \[\begin{align} \frac{dy}{dx}&=4(x)^{-1}+\sin (x) \\ &=-4x^{-2}+\cos x \end{align}\]
