Kinematic Equations

Integral Relationship Equations

Velocity

In a general form
\[V_x(t)=\dot{x}(t)=\frac{dx(t)}{dt}\]
Expressed as an integral relating it to position
 \[\Delta x=x(t)-x(t_0)=\int_0^t \dot{x}t'dt'\]

Acceleration

In a general form
\[A_x(t)=\ddot{x}(t)=\frac{d^2 x(t)}{dt^2}\]
Expressed as an integral relating it to velocity
 \[\Delta \dot{x}=\dot{x}(t)-\dot{x}(t_0)=\int_{t_0}^{t} \ddot{x}(t')dt'\]

Working Kinematic Equations

Constant position Function detailing no movement \[x(t)=x_0\]

Uniform motion/constant velocity Function detailing when movement is present, but there is no change in speed \[x(t)=x_0 +\dot{x}_0 \Delta t\]

Using an integral relationship
\[x(t)=x(t_0)+\int_{t_0}^{t} \dot{x}(t')dt\]

Constant acceleration Functions detailing when acceleration is nonzero but not changing

As a function of velocity
\[\dot{x}(t)=\dot{x}_0+\ddot{x}_0\Delta t\]
As a function of velocity when time is unknown
 \[\dot{x}(t_f)^2=x(t_0)^2 +2\ddot{x}_0 \Delta x\]
As a function of position
 \[x(t)=x_0+\dot{x}_0 \Delta x+\frac{1}{2}\ddot{x}_0\Delta t^2\]

Free fall Function detailing a special case of constant acceleration. Remember, the gravity constant is: \(g=9.81\frac{m}{s^2}\)

Displayed as gravity
\[\ddot{y}_0=-g\]
As a function of position
 \[y(t)=y_0+\dot{y}_0 \Delta t-\frac{1}{2}g\Delta t^2\]
