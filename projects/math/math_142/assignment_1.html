MATH 142 Assignment 1

  1. Find the area between the curves \(x=y^2-4y\) and \(x=2y-y^2\)
    2. Points of intersection
     \[ \begin{align} y^2-4y&=2y-y^2 \\ 2y^2-6y&=0 \\ 2y(y-3)&=0 \\ y=0 &\text{ and } y=3 \end{align} \]
    Finding area (by integral)
     \[ \begin{align} A&=\int_0^3 [(y^2-4y)-(2y-y^2)]dy \\ &=\int_0^3 [y^2-4y-2y+y^2]dy \\ &=\int_0^3 [2y^2-6y]dy \\ &=\left[\frac{2y^3}{3}-\frac{6y^2}{2}\right]_0^3 \rightarrow \left[\frac{2y^3}{3}-3y^2\right]_0^3\\ &=\left[\frac{2(3)^3}{3}-\frac{6(3)^2}{2}\right]-\left[\frac{2(0)^3}{3}-\frac{6(0)^2}{2}\right] \\ &=[-9]-[0]\rightarrow\|-9\| \\ \text{area }&=9\text{ units}^2 \end{align} \]
  2. Find the volume of the solid generated by rotating the area between \(y=\frac{1}{2}x^2\) and \(y=\frac{1}{2}\sqrt{x}\) on \(0\leq x\leq 1\) about the line \(y=3\). Show all work!
    3. \[ \begin{align} V&=\int_0^1 \left[3\pi -\pi\left(\frac{1}{2}x^2\right)-\pi\left(\frac{1}{2}\sqrt{x}\right)\right]dx \\ &=\left[\pi\left(\frac{3x}{1}\right)-\pi\left(\frac{1}{2}\times\frac{x^3}{3}\right)-\pi\left(\frac{1}{2}\times\frac{x^{\frac{3}{2}}}{\frac{3}{2}}\right)\right]_0^1 \\ &=\left[3\pi x-\pi\left(\frac{x^3}{6}\right)-\pi\left(\frac{x^{\frac{3}{2}}}{3}\right)\right]_0^1 \\ &=\left[3\pi (1)-\pi\left(\frac{(1)^3}{6}\right)-\pi\left(\frac{(1)^{\frac{3}{2}}}{3}\right)\right]-[0] \\ &=\left[3\pi -\frac{\pi}{6}-\frac{\pi}{3}\right]-[0] \\ &=\left[\frac{18\pi}{6}-\frac{\pi}{6}-\frac{2\pi}{6}\right]-[0] \\ \text{volume }&=\frac{15\pi}{6}\text{ units}^3 \end{align} \]
  3. Find the volume of the solid generated by rotating the area between the curve \(y=x-x^2\) and the \(x\)-axis about the \(y\)-axis.
    4. Points of intersection
     \[ \begin{align} 0&=x-x^2 \\ 0&=x(1-x) \\ y=0 &\text{ and } y=1 \end{align} \]
    Finding area (by integral)
     \[ \begin{align} V&=\int_0^1 \left[2\pi x(x-x^2)\right]dx \\ &=\int_0^1 \left(2\pi x^2-2\pi x^3\right)dx \\ &=\left[\frac{2\pi x^3}{3}-\frac{\pi x^4}{2}\right]_0^1 \\ &=\left[\frac{2\pi (1)^3}{3}-\frac{\pi (1)^4}{2}\right]-[0] \\ &=\left[\frac{4\pi}{6}-\frac{3\pi}{6}\right]-[0] \\ \text{volume }&=\frac{\pi}{6}\text{ units}^3 \end{align} \]
