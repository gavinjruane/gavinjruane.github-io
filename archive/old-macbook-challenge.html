Using a 2009 MacBook as my daily computer 17 Nov 2021

Yes, you read the title right; I use a 2009 polycarbonate unibody MacBook (yes, not the Air or Pro) with OS X 10.9 as my daily computer. I know; you probably have so mnay questions like: why? just why? I promise that I'll answer them later!

Why I put myself through this

Could it be the nostalgia? Maybe. Or just the beautiful design of the polycarbonate MacBook? Uh, yeah?!

No, but seriously: why do I use a Mac from 2009 with a version of OS X (ahh, remember the days before macOS?) that's from 2013? There are a number of reasons, but I'll list the main ones below. (Full disclosure: these are my opinions and definitely not facts.)

  • Older versions of OS X (Aqua!) looked and felt way better than Apple's recent attempts at macOS.
  • I like to show up to school with an ancient Mac while everyone else is walking around with their new MacBook Pro M1 Max and iPad Pro combination.
  • The design of the MacBook is so much cooler as well as more versatile.
  • I'm in the middle of a chellenge in which I only use Mac OS (any version) because I'm normally a Windows user.
  • There is a SuperDrive.

Okay, so that is my list of the main reasons why I use my MacBook. Let me elaborate!

Older versions of OS X are better

No, it's true, it's true! While Big Sur and Monterey drastically improved the interface of the Mac OS, nothing can beat the beauty and art of Aqua. I thought that Yosemite's overhaul back in the day was so cool, but as I reflect upon it now, Apple really did not put enough effort into making the new user interface feel natural.

Yes, versions 10.10-10.15 of OS X/macOS are just plain ugly.

Now, confession: I am using 10.9.5 Mavericks, which includes the worst rendition of Aqua since 10.0. But, it still looks way better than the latest OS this computer can run (High Sierra).
