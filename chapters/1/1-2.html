Local linearity 18 May 2022

Key ideas

  • A tangent line and its function are basically the same near the point of tangency.
  • Linear equation math is easier than functional equation math.
  • A normal line is a line perpendicular to another line
    • To get the slope of a normal line: change the slope to the opposite sign and its reciprocal
    • \(-3 \to +\frac{1}{2}\)

Sample problems

  1. Find the line tangent to \(f(x)=x^4-x^3-2x^2+1\) at \(x=-1\) and use it to approximate f(-0.9).

    2. X-coordinate

    \[\begin{align} f(-1)&=(-1)^4-(-1)^3+2(-1)^2+1 \\ &=1 \end{align}\]

    Slope

    \[\begin{align} f'(x)&=4x^3-3x^2-4x \\ &=4(-1)^3-3(-1)^2-4(-1) \\ &=-3 \end{align}\]

    Approximation

    \[\begin{align} y-1&=-3(x+1) \\ y-1&=-3(-0.9+1) \\ y-1&==0.3 \\ y&=0.7 \\ f(-0.9)&=0.7 \end{align}\]
  2. Find the line tangent to \(f(x)=\sqrt{15+e^{2x}}\) at \(x=0\) and use it to approximate f(0.02).

    3. X-coordinate

    \[\begin{align} f(0)&=\sqrt{15+e^{2(0)}} \\ &=4 \end{align}\]

    Slope

    \[\begin{align} f'(x)&=\frac{1}{2}(15+e^{2x})^{-\frac{1}{2}}(e^{2x}\times 2) \\ &=\frac{1}{2}(15+e^{2(0)})(2e^{2(0)}) \\ &=\frac{1}{4} \end{align}\]

    Approximation

    \[\begin{align} y-4&=\frac{1}{4}(x-0) \\ y-4&=\frac{1}{4}(0.02-0) \\ y-4&=0.005 \\ y&=4.005 \\ f(0.02)&=4.005 \end{align}\]
  3. Find the line tangent to \(f(x)=2x^3-9x^2-12x\) where \(f'(x)-12\)
