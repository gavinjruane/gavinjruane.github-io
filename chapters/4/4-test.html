  1. If \(g'(x)=3xe^{2x}+5\), then g(x) has a point of inflection at:

    2. c. \(x=-\frac{1}{2}\)

    This is because there is a minimum at \((-0.5, 4.4)\) on the derivative graph. A minimum on the derivative graph correlates with a point of inflection on the main graph.

  2. Given the functions \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\) that are both continuous and differentiable, and that they have values given on the table below: at \(x=8\), \(g(x)\) has a...

    3. b. Relative Minimum

    This is because of the following reasons: first, \(g'(x{\) at \(x=8\) is zero, which means that there is either a minimum or a maximum. The \(g''(x)\) value, \(4\), shows us that there is a minimum because it is a positive number.
