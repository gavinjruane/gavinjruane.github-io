Definite integrals and u-substitution 16 Nov 2021

Steps to evaluating a definite integral with u-substitution

  1. Recognize you are trying to integrate a product.
  2. Identify the interior function and call it \(u\).
  3. Find \(du\) from \(u\); introduce constants if necessary.
  4. Change integral boundaries to match what \(u\) would equal.
  5. Substitute \(u\), \(du\), and boundary numbers.
  6. Integrate.
  7. Plug boundaries in and subtract; do not return to \(x\).

Key ideas

  • Change the boundaries with a \(u\)-substitution.
  • With definite integrals, don't return from \(u\) to the original.
  • If the boundaries are equal, the answer is \(0\).
