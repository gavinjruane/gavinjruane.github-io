4-1 Review of Linear Functions

Formulas

Point Slope: \(y-y_{1}=m(x-x_{1})\)
Slope: \(\frac{y_{2}-y_{1}}{x_{2}-x_{1}}\)
Standard: \(Ax+By=C\)
Slope-Intercept: \(y=mx+b\)
Intercept: \(\frac{x}{a}+\frac{y}{b}=1\)

Vocabulary

Y-intercept: the point where \(x=0\)
zero: the point where \(y=0\); root, x-intercept (must be real)

Slopes and Linear Relationships

\(\parallel\) parallel: same slope
\(\perp\) perpendicular: opposite reciprocal
-- horizontal: slope \(=0\)
\(\mid\) vertical: does not exist

4-2 Review of Quadratic Functions

Formulas

Standard quadratic: \(y=ax^2+bx+c\)
Vertex quadratic: \(y=a(x-h)^2+k\)
Comparing conic sections: \((x-h)^2=4c(y-k)\)
Line of symmetry: \(x=h\)

4-3 Finding Complete Graphs of Polynomials

Vocabulary

complete graph: calculator showing zeros and extreme points
extreme points: high and low points on a graph relative and absolute (minimums and maximums)
degree: highest power of x in a quadratic like \(x^5+2x^4-47x^2+1\), 5 is the highest power
extreme value: y-value of extreme point
range: all possible y-values
